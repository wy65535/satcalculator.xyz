  • Identify areas where you need to improve
  • Track your progress through practice tests
  • Make informed decisions about retaking the test

Our calculator and educational resources are designed to demystify the SAT scoring process and help you achieve your best possible score.

How Our Calculator Works

Our SAT Score Calculator uses the same conversion methodology employed by the College Board:

  1. Raw Score Calculation: We count your correct answers in each section (there's no penalty for wrong answers)
  2. Test Score Conversion: Raw scores are converted to test scores (10-40 scale) for Reading and Writing
  3. Section Score Calculation: Test scores are combined and scaled to create section scores (200-800 for EBRW and Math)
  4. Total Score: Section scores are added together for your total SAT score (400-1600)

All conversions use official College Board tables to ensure accuracy.

Test Preparation Tips

Based on successful test-takers, here are key strategies for SAT preparation:

  • Start Early: Begin preparing several months before your test date
  • Use Official Materials: Practice with real SAT questions from the College Board
  • Take Full-Length Tests: Simulate test conditions to build stamina and time management
  • Analyze Your Mistakes: Understand why you got questions wrong
  • Focus on Weak Areas: Spend more time on sections where you score lowest
  • Build Core Skills: Strengthen fundamental math, reading, and grammar skills
  • Learn Test Strategies: Understand question patterns and answering techniques
  • Stay Consistent: Regular practice is more effective than cramming
